Observation 01

You are in a near-empty lane.

POTS affects an estimated 1 to 3 million people in the US alone. The r/POTS community on Reddit has over 150,000 members. Dysautonomia International reports diagnostic wait times averaging 4+ years. That's a massive audience that has been medically underserved, dismissed, and left without adapted fitness resources for years.

And yet the number of Instagram creators doing what you do - lived-experience POTS fitness, not just awareness content - can be counted on one hand.

You are not competing for space. You are establishing the category. The work now is discoverability and format optimization, not differentiation - you already have that.

Observation 02

The highest-value content format in your niche is underused.

In the chronic illness education space, save rates on educational carousels are dramatically higher than most fitness niches - because the audience is research-literate and saves content to share with doctors, family members, and people they're trying to help understand their condition.

A carousel titled "Why your heart rate spikes just from standing up" doesn't just get engagement. It becomes a resource someone texts to their cardiologist.

Carousel content built around mechanism-level explanations has outsized organic reach in this niche. That format may be underdeveloped relative to the knowledge you already have.

Observation 03

Brand partnership territory is almost completely open.

Electrolyte brands, compression garment companies, and heart rate monitor brands are the most natural fits in the POTS fitness space. Almost none of them are running visible creator partnerships with accounts that speak authentically to the dysautonomia community.

A creator with genuine lived-experience credibility is worth significantly more to these brands than a generic wellness influencer. The positioning angle writes itself: the only fitness creator who uses these products because she actually has to.

Sample 01 - Reel Caption

Angle: the fitness industry wasn't built for your body - and that's the industry's failure, not yours

okay so genuine question: why does every fitness video start with "stand with feet shoulder-width apart" like... that's the whole problem. my heart rate doesn't care that you labeled it "low intensity." the moment i'm upright for more than a few minutes, it's already doing what it wants. for years i thought that was just me failing at exercise. it wasn't. it was exercise failing people with POTS. the CHOP protocol doesn't start with standing. the Levine protocol doesn't start with standing. the actual research doesn't start with standing. the fitness industry just never got the memo. so here's what my workouts actually look like: on the floor. recumbent. sometimes rowing. sometimes strength work lying down. not because i'm lazy. because that's how you build cardiovascular tolerance with a dysautonomia diagnosis without crashing for three days after. if you've been avoiding exercise because every video sends you into a flare - it's not you. the content was built for a different body. 💙 save this for the next time someone tells you to "just push through it"
Sample 02 - Educational Carousel (7 slides)

Angle: why standing up is hard with POTS - the mechanism explained simply

Slide 01 - Hook

why your heart rate spikes just from standing up (a very unsatisfying explanation from your own body)

Slide 02

when most people stand up, the body automatically redirects blood flow upward to keep the brain and heart supplied. with POTS, that signal doesn't work the way it should. blood pools in the legs and lower body instead of moving up.

Slide 03

your heart's response? beat faster. a lot faster. trying to compensate for the drop in blood returning to it. that's not anxiety. that's physics.

Slide 04

this is why "just stand up slowly" helps a little but doesn't fix it. the problem isn't speed. it's that the whole autonomic signaling system isn't doing its job.

Slide 05

and it's why recumbent and supine exercise actually helps. when you're horizontal, gravity isn't pulling blood away from where it needs to be. your heart gets to work with a full tank.

Slide 06

over time, building that cardiovascular base lying down translates to better upright tolerance. not immediately. not overnight. but that's the direction.

Slide 07 - Close

if this finally explained something you've been feeling for years but couldn't name - 💙 save this for the next time someone tells you exercise is "just exercise" follow for POTS-friendly fitness content that starts where your body actually is
